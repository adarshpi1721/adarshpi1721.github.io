Interests

Philosophy

I read ethics, existentialism, and philosophy of religion—subjects that force you to examine assumptions and build a worldview from first principles. I enjoy tracing how thinkers like Epictetus, Aristotle, and Dostoevsky reach their conclusions.

Philosophy has shaped my approach to setbacks, habits, and the ethical side of design— it’s practical thinking about how to live and how to act well in research and life.

Tennis

I’ve played since childhood. Tennis is where strategy, rhythm, and focus come together. I enjoy the solitude of court practice and the precision of each point.

Federer is timeless to watch; Sinner is a current favorite. It's probably my favorite sport to watch.

Reading

I read widely, with a soft spot for classic literature that explores human motives and moral complexity. Books let you step into another mind—an experience I value more than almost any other.

I prefer books that change how you see people or practice, especially those that linger and shape decisions long after the last page.

Sketching

I moved from semi-realism to anatomy studies; sketching forces careful observation of form and proportion. It’s a practice that enhances patience and attention to detail.

Sketching complements engineering—both reward repeated practice and a habit of looking more closely.

Piano

Piano was a big part of my childhood. I learned Für Elise on an old keyboard and still play pieces I enjoy. These days it’s mostly for pleasure, but it remains a comforting creative outlet.

Music is one of the few things that brings me back to a quiet, reflective state.
