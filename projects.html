Projects

Morphing Foils — Biomimicry of Fish Locomotion

Ongoing research combining chordwise trailing-edge morphing with pitching–heaving motion to replicate fish-like locomotion and improve propulsion. Using ALE-based 2D models and DMD wake analysis, I studied how morph timing affects thrust, efficiency, and wake structure. This work led to thrust increases of ~35% and efficiency gains of ~12%, and was presented at FMFP ’24 and submitted to Physics of Fluids.

Ornithopter

Designed and built a dual-crank active flapping ornithopter inspired by bird flight. After achieving controlled, repeatable kinematics, I added trailing-edge morphing to boost thrust and stability. Result: a stable 7-second flight and lots of lessons about FSI in the real world.

Suppression of Vortex-Induced Vibrations (VIV)

I worked on passive VIV suppression techniques while helping build and validate a low-speed wind tunnel (up to ~96% accuracy). I developed an OpenFOAM solver for 2D VIV, explored passive devices like splitter plates and control cylinders, and compared simulation trends with experimental data.
