Projects

Ornithopter

The project that started it all!

Designed and built an ornithopter (an aircraft that replicated bird motion) using a dual crank mechanism for flapping. Once we had a stable and repeatable flight, I added a simple trailing-edge morphing element to improve thrust. We achieved a controlled 7-second flight!

Morphing Foils and Biomimicry of Fish Locomotion

The project that led me to become a researcher.

Ongoing research work aimed at replicating the motion used by fish. I analyzed different configurations and found out that morphing can help increase thrust by around 35% and efficiency by around 12%. This was later extended by analyzing these morphing foils in a tandem configuration, which helped improve the propulsive coefficients even further.

Suppression of Vortex-Induced Vibrations (VIV)

The project that introduced me to experimental work.

Worked on passive VIV control for circular cylinders. I helped setup and validate a low-speed wind tunnel (up to ~96% agreement with reference data) and developed a 2D VIV solver in OpenFOAM. The project compared different passive devices and studied how they modify lift, drag, and vibration response by changing the wake.
