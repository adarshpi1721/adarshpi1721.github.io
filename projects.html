Morphing Foils — Biomimicry of Fish Locomotion

My ongoing research on fish-inspired propulsion and morphing foils. I built ALE-based 2D models, performed DMD wake analysis, and studied how morph timing affects thrust and efficiency. This work (FMFP ’24 + PoF submission) showed up to ~31% thrust increase and ~12% efficiency improvement.

Ornithopter

Designed and built a dual-crank active flapping ornithopter inspired by bird flight. After achieving controlled, repeatable kinematics, I added trailing-edge morphing to boost thrust and stability. Result: a stable 7-second flight and lots of lessons about FSI in the real world.

Suppression of Vortex-Induced Vibrations (VIV)

Worked on passive VIV suppression techniques for cylinders. I helped set up and validate a low-speed wind tunnel (up to ~96% accuracy), built an OpenFOAM-based 2D VIV solver, and compared simulation trends with experimental data across control geometries.
