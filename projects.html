Projects

Ornithopter

The project that started it all!

Designed and built a bird-inspired ornithopter with a mechanically driven flapping mechanism. Once we had a stable and repeatable flight, I added a simple trailing-edge morphing element to improve thrust and stability. We achieved a controlled 7-second flight and learned how sensitive real hardware is to timing, stiffness, and mass distribution.

Morphing Foils and Biomimicry of Fish Locomotion

The project that led me to become a researcher.

Ongoing work on fish-like propulsion using trailing-edge morphing on a pitching–heaving foil. I built ALE-based 2D simulations and used DMD on the wake to understand how morph timing and amplitude affect thrust, efficiency, and vortex structure. In the best cases, we see roughly 31% higher thrust and a cleaner reverse von Kármán wake.

Suppression of Vortex-Induced Vibrations (VIV)

The project that introduced me to experimental work.

Worked on passive VIV control for circular cylinders. I helped build and validate a low-speed wind tunnel (up to ~96% agreement with reference data) and developed a 2D VIV solver in OpenFOAM. The project compared different passive devices and studied how they modify lift, drag, and vibration response by changing the wake.
