Ornithopter

The project that started it all!

Designed and built an ornithopter (an aircraft that replicated bird motion) using a dual crank mechanism for flapping. Once we had a stable and repeatable motion, I added a simple trailing-edge morphing element to improve thrust and life. With this, we achieved a controlled 7-second flight!

Morphing Foils and Biomimicry of Fish Locomotion

The project that led me to become a researcher.

Ongoing research work aimed at replicating the motion used by fish. I analyzed different configurations and found out that morphing (adding flexibility to foils) can help increase thrust by around 35% and efficiency by around 12%. This was later extended by analyzing these morphing foils in fish schools (using a simplified tandem model), which helped improve the propulsive coefficients even further.

Suppression of Vortex-Induced Vibrations (VIV)

The project that introduced me to experimental work.

Worked on passive VIV control for circular cylinders. I helped set up and validate a low-speed wind tunnel (up to ~96% accuracy for validation tests) and developed a 2D VIV solver in OpenFOAM. The goal was to come up with unique passive additions to a cylinder to analyze how it affects the vibrations on the cylinder, ultimately trying to suppress these vibrations.
