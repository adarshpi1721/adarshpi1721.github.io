Ytterbium Beam Collimation — Rarefied Gas Dynamics

Summary — For my thesis at IIT Bombay I study how rarefied gas effects (through the Knudsen number) influence the collimation of ytterbium beams. The work connects gas-kinetic behavior with beam shaping needs in precision imaging and instrumentation.

Approach

I use models that account for molecular mean free path and run parametric simulations over background pressure, nozzle geometry, and source conditions. The idea is to see how these parameters affect directionality and flux distribution and to identify combinations that lead to well-collimated beams.

Key observations

Near transitional regimes, small geometric tweaks can noticeably change beam spread and uniformity. This suggests that careful nozzle design and operating conditions can provide significant gains before relying on downstream optical corrections.

What I learned

The project pushed me to think beyond standard continuum CFD and work with rarefied models. It has also been a good exercise in balancing analytical approximations, computational cost, and the practical requirements of an experimental setup.
