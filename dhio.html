Conduction of Graphene and Flow Around Drone Rotors

Summary — My first research internship, where I got hands-on experience with industry-level software. Primarily, my work focused on simulating various properties of graphene nanoribbons, like phonon dispersion plots and optimized geometry configurations. I used the software ASAP to simulate these nanoribbons, and for simulating the flow around drone rotors, I used Particleworks, which is a meshless software.

What I learnt

Approaching the topic of quantum physics was quite new to me. Unlike fluid mechanics, my background in mechanical engineering meant I didn't necessarily have all the resources to fully comprehend wave functions and DFT. This meant I spent the initial days of my time learning about the basics of DFT, how it is actually implemented in the software, and what results we get out of it. I had to perform hand calculations, as was advised by my mentor, which meant I had to understand some aspects of the software at a fundamental level.

Key observations

The rotor simulations suggested small geometry changes that reduce peak tip velocities and potentially lower noise. The conduction studies showed how ribbon width and edge characteristics influence effective thermal transport, which is important for thermal management design.

What I learned

The internship was a good reality check on how simulations are used in practice: reliability, turnaround time, and clear communication are as important as accuracy. I also got comfortable adapting from academic solvers to industry tools and workflows.
