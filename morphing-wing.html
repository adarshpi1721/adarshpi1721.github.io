Morphing Foils — Biomimicry of Fish Locomotion

Image Placeholder

Summary — This project merges two interconnected threads: using chordwise trailing-edge morphing to actively tune a flapping foil’s camber, and understanding how fish-like tandem arrangements exploit wakes. The work uses ALE-based 2D simulations, DMD wake analysis, and targeted bench checks. Results show morphing can raise thrust by ~31% and improve propulsive efficiency by ~12% in tested cases.

Approach

I used a deliberately simple ALE 2D modeling framework to keep physics interpretable. Key steps were:

  • Define a baseline pitching–heaving kinematic; add morphing as an extra DOF (amplitude + phase).
  • Perform parameter sweeps (morph amplitude, phase offset, reduced frequency) and compute time-averaged thrust, lift, and power coefficients.
  • Run tandem-foil cases to examine downstream responses and use DMD on snapshots to extract coherent wake structures.

Key findings

- Morph timing matters: morphing that reinforces thrust-producing vortices on the downstroke yields net gains.
- Gains were robust across moderate Reynolds number ranges sampled; tandem configurations showed potential for further boost by placing a morphing foil downstream of a flapping leader.
- DMD showed that morphing shifts dominant wake modes and phase relationships, explaining the robustness of thrust trends.

Publications & status

- Presented: Propulsion of Combined Morphing–Pitching–Heaving Foil, FMFP ’24 (Adarsh Pai, Vaibhav Joshi, Shibu Clement).
- Manuscript: Active flexibility via chordwise trailing-edge morphing can enhance the propulsive performance of a flapping foil — submitted to Physics of Fluids.

Practical challenges

Numerically, mesh deformation and timestep selection required careful tuning to avoid instabilities from the moving trailing edge. Experimentally, actuation and power trade-offs (more actuators = more weight & power draw) must be balanced against aerodynamic gains.

What I learned

This project taught me to marry interpretable simulation design with physical intuition. Often small timing changes flip outcomes — focusing on mechanism (wake timing, vortex formation) made the difference between a convincing result and an ambiguous one.
